Brief History of Electric Power

Thomas Edison's first electric power plant generated 110-volt direct current (DC) for lighting and variable-speed machinery in nearby buildings.

Then Nikola tesla invented the alternating current (AC) induction motor and transformer. With constant AC frecuency, an introduction motor's speed cannot vary, but is cheaper and more durable than a DC motor.

But not forever. In the middle of the 20th century, computer began consuming progressively more DC power.

DC power, computer control, an the internet are helping the power grid accept variable renewable resource like wind and solar.

* 1882: Edison's Pearl street DC power plant in New York
* 1884: Tesla invents closed-core AC transformer in Budapest
* 1886: Thorenberg AC power plant in Lucene
* 1985: Insulated-gate bipolar transistor by Toshiba
* 2012: UCSD 42-megawatt microgrid in southern California
